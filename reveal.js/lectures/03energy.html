Lesson 3.1

Energy

Matter and energy are related by the E = mc2 equation.

Energy can be potential or kinetic energy.

The kinetic energy of an object is the energy from its movement.

The potential energy of an object is the energy from its position.

Hot Rocks Lab

Materials

  • Hot Rocks(marbles)
  • 50 mL beaker
  • graduated cylinder
  • Thermometer
  • Safety goggles and lab apron

Procedure

  • Use the graduated cylinder to put 20 mL of water in a 50 mL beaker.
  • Measure the temperature of the water and the hot rocks using the infrared thermometer. Record both values in the table below.
  • Pour the water into the cup with the hot rocks.
  • Wait 1 minute and then measure the temperature of the water and the rocks in the cup. Record the value in the table below.

Procedure Cont.

  • Record the initial water temperature.
  • Set the food on fire and observe the water temperature as the food burns.
  • When the food is finished burning, record the final water temperature.
  • After the food has cooled, weigh the remaining pieces of the food on the same paper used before.
